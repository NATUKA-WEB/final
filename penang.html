TOP DESTINATIONS

STREET ART

Streets were painted by the Lithuanian artist Ernest Zacharevich with humor and sarcasm. Unique painting walls mimicking life in the city and the wrought-iron caricatures with anecdotal and funny descriptions pf the street that they adorn.

FORT CORNVALIS

Fort Cornwallis is one of historical place in Penang made by captain of British army Francis Light Beckon to protect British royal artillery from possible French attacks. The fort was completed in 1810 and later became administrative base for British troops in Penang. Some of the cell rooms now are galleries displaying historical facts, artifacts and conservative efforts.

BATU FERINGH BEACH

One of Penang’s most popular destinations (in fact, it’s the second most popular destination after Georgetown), Batu Ferringhi consists of a long stretch of soft, white sandy beach along a winding road named Jalan Batu Ferringhi, filled with a host of accommodation and dining options. Its night market is quite legendary, while its waters are popular spots for a range of water sports activities such as jet-skiing, parasailing and windsurfing

GEORG TOWN

ISLE OF SUNRISE

If tropical climate is your vocation or in a cold winter you want to enjoy with an ocean sparkling sun rays, you cannot miss Penang, for it will help your dream make true.

Penang is special island, where strong Asian and subtle European culture cross each other. Unique eastern architecture, Asian culture and updated historical reality will force visitors to come back to Penang once more.

Fort Cornwallis will guide you in the past days of Malaysia. visually presents history and condition of Penang in 18th century. “Little India” and “China town” will present the visitors coexistence of two different culture with their tradition, cuisine, believes and art.

The city hall, Batik painting museum and Jubila clock tower are Penang’s pure modern history. Very creative street art is telling interesting stories via pictures. “Penang state museum art & gallery”, “National Park” and unique botanic garden are visual proofs of rich natural wonders of Penang. As the saying goes “ROME DO AS ROMANS DO” Penang Street food also works in the same way. It’s a combination of Chinese, Malay and Indian fast food. All served in a matter of minutes.

OUR GALLERY

STREETS OF GEORGE TOWN

Experience the unique charms inside George Town city, where even streets are telling stories about Penang Island. And even more, corners do have stories to tell or two. Old streets with some newly reconstructed streets are making a new face of Penang with an old charm. Walk inside the streets and discover a true heart of Penang with its different multicultural, Chinese, Indian and native Malay stocks. Better join. guided walking tours or do green wheels via a bicycle or trishaw ride to discover more and know more.

KEK LOK SI TEMPLE

Huge bells of the temple that rivals even those of Notre Dame speaks of temple’s glory while fearsome stone dragons and lions stand guard over the holy sanctuary and its devotee. temple bears testament of some 200 years of pious devotion. Temple is called as “KEK LOK SI” . During 1800 the temple was the most magical Chinese structures in northern of malaya which could boast of both carotenes and hokkien elements in its amazing construction.

CITY HALL

City hall is placed in the center of Georg Town. An amazing building makes a special view of a city. Mixed with different culture, now the building is currently occupied by the local council. With its British Palladian architecture featuring amazing Corinthian columns and early Middle Ages design created very emotional background for the visitors. City hall was opened in 1906.
